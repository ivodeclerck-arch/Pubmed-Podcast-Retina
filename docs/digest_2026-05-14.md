### Intraocular Neoplasm Suggested by Ocular POCUS Examination.
_Hernández-Restrepo F et al. — POCUS journal_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42131542/)

**Spoken:** A 30-year-old woman presented with a new-onset headache, nausea, vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia, and right nasal hemianopsia. Her symptoms were non-specific, making it challenging to diagnose the underlying condition. That's when ocular point of care ultrasound, or POCUS, came into play. A team from the Emergency Department at Fundación Instituto Neurológico de Colombia in Medellín, Colombia, reported this case, highlighting the importance of ocular POCUS in the diagnostic approach.

The patient's ocular POCUS examination revealed a hyperechoic lesion adhered to the vascular layer that didn't move with eye movements. This finding was critical in suspecting a neoplastic lesion. Further evaluation by ophthalmology confirmed choroidal melanoma, a malignant lesion of the uveal tract, and extension studies showed no metastases. The patient underwent enucleation, and the authors noted that timely diagnosis is essential for intraocular neoplastic lesions, as they are highly invasive.

Choroidal melanoma is relatively rare, but it's the most common ocular melanoma, occurring more frequently in younger women. The clinical presentation is usually non-specific, making it difficult to diagnose in the emergency department. A well-conducted physical exam and fundoscopic evaluation are critical, and ocular POCUS can complement the physical exam by evaluating the posterior chamber.

The authors noted that ocular POCUS is relatively easy to perform, and the eye's high liquid content provides an excellent medium for sound transmission. This allows detection of lesions that appear hyperechoic within the aqueous humor. They also mentioned that the learning curve for emergency physicians is relatively short, as this examination is quick, easy to perform, and non-invasive.

The clinical implications of this case are significant. Intraocular neoplastic lesions are rare in emergency settings, but for patients with non-specific symptoms, physical examination can be supplemented by ocular POCUS. Detecting non-mobile solid lesions can suggest neoplasia, and timely diagnosis is essential to prevent progression and metastatic dissemination. The main treatments for choroidal melanoma are enucleation and chemotherapy. Overall, this case highlights the importance of ocular POCUS in the diagnostic approach to patients with non-specific symptoms and its potential to improve patient outcomes.

**Abstract:** We report the case of a 30-year-old woman with no relevant medical history who presented with a new-onset headache associated with nausea, vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia, and right nasal hemianopsia. Ocular point of care ultrasound (POCUS) examination revealed a hyperechoic lesion adhered to the vascular layer that did not move with eye movements. The patient was referred to ophthalmology, where choroidal melanoma was confirmed. Extension studies showed no metastases, and enucleation was performed. This case highlighted the importance of ocular POCUS in the diagnostic approach to this patient.

### One-Year Tumor Volume Regression Predicts Survival After I-125 Plaque Brachytherapy for Posterior Uveal Melanoma.
_Dave N et al. — American journal of ophthalmology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42128079/)

**Spoken:** This study from the Hamilton Eye Institute, Department of Ophthalmology, University of Tennessee, in Memphis, Tennessee, looked at the prognostic significance of tumor volume regression after brachytherapy for posterior uveal melanoma. The goal was to see if the extent of tumor shrinkage at one year could predict patient survival.

The researchers conducted a retrospective analysis of over 1,100 patients with uveal melanoma who were treated with low-dose-rate iodine-125 episcleral plaque brachytherapy between 1984 and 2022. They had ultrasound measurements of their tumors at diagnosis and one year after treatment, and complete survival data was available.

The key finding was that patients with intermediate tumor regression - between 33% and 75% - had better overall survival than those with delayed or accelerated regression. In fact, 94.4% of patients showed some degree of tumor volume reduction, with a mean decrease from 336.3 mm³ to 129.5 mm³ by one year. The study used Kaplan-Meier survival analysis to compare outcomes among regression groups, and the association between tumor regression and survival was significant even when tumors were stratified by size and clinical stage.

The authors suggest that one-year tumor regression metrics could be a practical tool for risk stratification and patient counseling. However, it's worth noting that the study had some limitations, including its retrospective design and reliance on ultrasound measurements. Still, the findings support the idea that tumor volume regression at one year is a significant prognostic biomarker for overall survival in uveal melanoma patients. This information could help ophthalmologists and oncologists make more informed treatment decisions and provide more accurate guidance to patients.

**Abstract:** OBJECTIVE: To evaluate the prognostic significance of one-year tumor volume regression (TVR) following low-dose-rate iodine-125 episcleral plaque brachytherapy (EPBT) in patients with uveal melanoma. DESIGN: Retrospective single-center clinical cohort study. PARTICIPANTS: A total of 1,180 patients with uveal melanoma treated with low-dose-rate iodine-125 EPBT delivered at 52.8 cGy/h over 168 hours at a large academic medical center with an ocular oncology service between 1984 and 2022. Patients had tumor ultrasound measurements at diagnosis and one-year follow-up, with complete survival data available. Patients without complete survival data or ultrasound measurements at either timepoint were excluded. MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES: TVR was calculated using ultrasound-derived ellipsoid modeling at baseline and 1 year. Patients were stratified into delayed (≤33.3%), intermediate (33.3-75%), and accelerated (≥75%) regression groups. Thresholds were selected using a log-rank test sweep across -100% to 100% regression in conjunction with locally weighted scatterplot smoothing (LOWESS) analysis. Inflection points were identified at 33.3% (p = 0.029) and 75% (p = 0.001). Kaplan-Meier (KM) survival analysis was used to compare overall survival (OS) among regression groups, with significance assessed by log-rank tests. RESULTS: Mean tumor volume decreased from 336.3 mm³ to 129.5 mm³ by one year, with 94.4% (N=1,114) showing volume reduction. KM survival analysis demonstrated that patients with delayed and accelerated tumor regression had worse OS than those with intermediate-range regression across the full cohort (p < 0.0001, p < 0.0001). This association was conserved when tumors were stratified by Collaborative Ocular Melanoma Study (COMS) size and American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) clinical stage guidelines. No difference was seen between the intermediate and accelerated regression groups for COMS small and cT1 tumors. CONCLUSION: TVR at one year following EPBT is a significant prognostic biomarker for OS in UM, with intermediate regression (33.33% to 75%) conferring the greatest survival benefit. These findings support one year tumor regression metrics as a practical tool for risk stratification and patient counseling.

### Reassessing the incidence of uveal melanoma in Germany: Insights from current cancer registry data.
_Lever M et al. — Cancer epidemiology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42127869/)

**Spoken:** Uveal melanoma, or UM, is a type of eye cancer that's most common in adults, and it's particularly prevalent in Europe. A team from the Department of Ophthalmology at University Hospital Essen in Germany took a closer look at just how often UM occurs in their country, using data from cancer registries.

The motivation here is that calculating the incidence of UM can be tricky, given current diagnostic and therapeutic practices. So, the researchers wanted to get a better estimate of UM incidence in Germany, and specifically in the state of North Rhine-Westphalia, which is the largest federal state.

To do this, they analyzed data from the German Centre for Cancer Registry Data, covering the years 2019 to 2021, and compared it to data from North Rhine-Westphalia's cancer registry for 2019 to 2022. They looked at a total of 2047 UM cases in Germany, and 830 cases in North Rhine-Westphalia. 

The mean age at diagnosis was 65 years, with men and women equally affected. Interestingly, they found that the age-standardized incidence rate in Germany was 7.4 per million person-years. But here's a striking finding: there was a lot of variation in incidence rates across different federal states, with the highest rate in Schleswig-Holstein at 16.6 per million person-years.

In North Rhine-Westphalia, the incidence rate was 6.2 per million person-years according to the German Centre for Cancer Registry Data, but 10.6 per million person-years according to the state's own cancer registry. This discrepancy suggests that the true incidence of UM in Germany might be higher than previously thought, likely similar to rates seen in Northern Europe, around 10-12 per million person-years.

The takeaway here is that determining UM incidence in Germany remains challenging, and the authors suspect that their dataset might not be entirely complete. This highlights the need for more accurate and comprehensive data to get a clearer picture of UM incidence, which is important for understanding and addressing this disease.

**Abstract:** BACKGROUND: Uveal melanoma (UM), the most common adult primary intraocular malignancy, has its highest frequency in Europe. Because of the current diagnostic and therapeutic practices, calculating UM incidence can be error prone. Using cancer registry data, the aim here was to estimate UM incidence in Germany and North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW), its largest federal state. METHODS: All UM cases from the German Centre for Cancer Registry Data (RKI) in 2019-2021 were analyzed. Data were compared to the 2019-2022 data of NRW's cancer registry. We calculated crude incidence and age-standardized rates (ASR) using the 2013 European standard population. RESULTS: Overall, 2047 German and 830 NRW UM cases were included. In both datasets, the mean age at diagnosis was 65 years; men and women were equally affected. Ciliary body and iris melanomas represented 11-13% of total cases. In Germany (RKI dataset), ASR was 7.4 per million person-years (pyrs). Highest ASR was in the North (Schleswig-Holstein: 16.6 per million pyrs), followed by North-Eastern federal states (ASR: 11-13 per million pyrs in Brandenburg, Berlin, Saxony-Anhalt, Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania), and ASR was 4-8 per million pyrs in the remaining federal states. ASR in NRW was 6.2 (RKI dataset) but 10.6 per million pyrs using the state's cancer registry data. CONCLUSION: Determining UM incidence in Germany remains challenging. High incidence variations between both datasets and German federal states point to a probable incompleteness of the RKI UM-dataset. Incidence is likely higher than previously assumed, but similar to rates observed in Northern Europe (around 10-12 per million pyrs).

### Network-based analysis reveals potential microRNA regulation of oncogenic pathways in SOX10-depleted uveal melanoma.
_Luan C et al. — Cellular and molecular life sciences : CMLS_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42120529/)

**Spoken:** This study comes from the Department of Dermatology at Universitätsklinikum Erlangen and Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg in Erlangen, Germany. The researchers are investigating the role of SOX10 in uveal melanoma, specifically how it affects microRNA regulation. SOX10 is a crucial transcription factor for melanocyte development and maintenance, and its dysregulation is linked to uveal melanoma initiation and progression.

The study aimed to characterize the microRNA regulatory landscape following SOX10 depletion in uveal melanoma cells. The researchers used a network-based approach, integrating mRNA and miRNA expression data from SOX10 wild-type and knockdown uveal melanoma cells. They identified 17 microRNAs with significant changes in regulatory activities following SOX10 knockdown. Notably, miR-34a, miR-25, miR-186, and miR-211 showed tumor-suppressive potential by targeting genes involved in uveal melanoma progression and metastasis.

The study found that SOX10 knockdown leads to massive changes in gene expression, with 2,222 up-regulated and 1,156 down-regulated protein-coding genes. The researchers highlighted that SOX10's regulatory activity is essential for maintaining the transcriptomic landscape of uveal melanoma cells. They also noted that the identified microRNAs and their target genes are involved in cancer hallmark pathways, including cell cycle progression, mTORC1 signaling, and fatty acid metabolism.

The authors suggest that their findings have clinical implications, as understanding the microRNA regulatory landscape in uveal melanoma could lead to the development of new therapeutic strategies. However, they also note that their study has limitations, as it is based on in vitro experiments and requires further validation in vivo. Additionally, the researchers acknowledge that the study focused on a specific cell line and that further studies are needed to confirm the findings in other uveal melanoma cell lines.

The study's results provide new insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying uveal melanoma and highlight the potential of targeting microRNAs for therapeutic purposes. However, further research is needed to translate these findings into clinical practice.

**Abstract:** SOX10 is essential for melanocyte development and maintenance and plays a critical role in uveal melanoma (UM) initiation and progression. While SOX10's transcriptional regulation of protein-coding genes is well characterized, its role on microRNA (miRNA) regulatory landscape in UM remains unexplored. Here, we employed network-based modeling to systematically characterize miRNA regulatory functions following SOX10 depletion in UM. First, we profiled mRNA and miRNA expression levels in SOX10 wild-type and knockdown UM cells. Then, we integrated the transcriptomic data, a UM network, and a Bayesian model to quantify miRNAs' regulatory activities and identify key miRNAs. Subsequently, we employed pathway enrichment analysis combined with literature mining to elucidate the functional roles of identified miRNAs through their target genes and associated signaling pathways in UM. We identified 17 miRNAs that show significant changes in regulatory activities following SOX10 knockdown in UM cells. These miRNAs regulate the expression of genes involved in cancer hallmark pathways, including cell cycle progression, mTORC1 signaling, and fatty acid metabolism. Notably, miR-34a, miR-25, miR-186, and miR-211 have tumor-suppressive potential by targeting genes involved in UM progression and metastasis. Our computational analysis suggests that SOX10 depletion in UM reshapes the miRNA regulatory landscape in a manner potentially consistent with tumor-suppressive activity, requiring experimental validation.

### Long-Term Local Control and Mortality After Transpupillary Thermotherapy of Small Uveal Melanomas.
_Fili M et al. — Clinical & experimental ophthalmology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42104514/)

**Spoken:** This study from the Department of Clinical Neuroscience, Division of Eye and Vision, Karolinska Institutet, Stockholm, Sweden, looks at the long-term effectiveness of transpupillary thermotherapy, or TTT, for treating small uveal melanomas. The goal here is to compare outcomes for patients who received TTT alone, TTT in combination with plaque brachytherapy, and plaque brachytherapy alone. 

The motivation behind this study seems to be the need to evaluate TTT, which is used for certain small choroidal melanocytic tumors, either on its own or alongside other treatments like plaque brachytherapy. The researchers wanted to see how these approaches stacked up in terms of local recurrence and mortality over the long term.

The study itself is a retrospective cohort analysis of 850 patients with choroidal melanocytic tumors who were treated at St. Erik Eye Hospital in Stockholm between 2008 and 2020. The patients were grouped based on their treatment: 48 received TTT alone, 105 got TTT plus plaque brachytherapy, and 697 had plaque brachytherapy alone.

The key findings here are that TTT alone was associated with significantly higher rates of local recurrence and melanoma-related mortality. For instance, at 15 years, the local recurrence-free survival rate was only 8% for those who received TTT alone, compared to 86% for those who had plaque brachytherapy alone. This suggests a notable difference in the effectiveness of these treatments over time.

The authors note that these findings have implications for how TTT is used in managing choroidal melanocytic tumors. They suggest that TTT might not be as effective as plaque brachytherapy in controlling the tumor and preventing mortality. However, they also mention the need for further studies to confirm these results in other cohorts before making any broad recommendations about changing treatment approaches. One point of concern is that among patients who experienced local recurrence, those treated with TTT had more subsequent recurrences, and the risk of extraocular extension increased with the number of TTT sessions. 

Overall, this study provides valuable insights into the long-term outcomes of different treatments for small uveal melanomas, highlighting the potential need for a re-evaluation of the role of TTT in treatment protocols.

**Abstract:** BACKGROUND: Transpupillary thermotherapy (TTT) is used for selected small choroidal melanocytic tumours, either as primary treatment or as an adjunct to plaque brachytherapy. We compared long-term local recurrence and mortality outcomes after primary TTT alone, plaque brachytherapy combined with TTT (brachy+TTT), and plaque brachytherapy alone (brachy alone). METHODS: This retrospective cohort study included 850 patients with choroidal melanocytic tumours treated with eye-preserving modalities at St. Erik Eye Hospital, Stockholm, Sweden, 2008-2020. Primary treatment was TTT alone (n = 48), brachy+TTT (n = 105), or brachy alone (n = 697). Outcomes included local recurrence, local recurrence with extraocular extension (EXE), and mortality. RESULTS: At 15 years, Kaplan-Meier local recurrence-free survival was 8% after TTT alone, 34% after brachy+TTT, and 86% after brachy alone (log-rank test for trend, p < 0.001). Disease-specific survival at 15 years was 46%, 85%, and 90%, respectively (p = 0.03). In multivariable Fine-Gray regression adjusting for American Joint Committee on Cancer stage, TTT alone was associated with a higher subdistribution hazard of local recurrence (sHR, 6.7; p < 0.001) and melanoma-related death (sHR, 3.9; p < 0.001). Among patients treated for local recurrence, subsequent recurrences occurred more frequently after TTT than after treatments involving brachytherapy. The risk of EXE increased with the number of TTT sessions. CONCLUSIONS: In this single-centre retrospective cohort, TTT was associated with higher rates of local recurrence and melanoma-related mortality compared with plaque brachytherapy. Future studies should assess whether similar results are observed in external long-term cohorts; such findings would inform whether the role of TTT in the management of choroidal melanocytic tumours warrants reconsideration.

**Spoken:** In the field of ophthalmic oncology, artificial intelligence is making significant strides, particularly in the diagnosis and treatment of conditions like retinoblastoma and uveal melanoma. The authors aimed to investigate how AI can enhance diagnostic paradigms in this field. The clinical implications are significant. However, the authors note that clinical adoption remains limited by the need for large, diverse datasets and prospective validation. **Abstract:** INTRODUCTION: Artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping diagnostic paradigms across oncology. Multicenter collaborations and integration of multimodal imaging are essential to move from proof-of-concept to real-world application. With continued development, AI can significantly enhance diagnostic precision, personalize care, and serve as a scalable model for cancer imaging in ophthalmology and beyond.

### Intravitreal delivery of LATS1 mRNA by lipid nanoparticles as an effective strategy for uveal melanoma therapy.
_Dong S et al. — Journal of controlled release : official journal of the Controlled Release Society_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41786044/)

**Spoken:** Uveal melanoma, a rare but aggressive eye cancer, has a key driver of its growth that's been identified as Yes-associated protein, or YAP. This protein is overactive in uveal melanoma because of a loss of certain tumor suppressor kinases, including LATS1. Restoring LATS1 activity could potentially slow down or stop the growth of this cancer.

Scientists from the Eye Research Center, Hangzhou Institute of Medicine, Chinese Academy of Sciences, have been exploring a new approach to treat uveal melanoma by targeting the LATS1/YAP axis. They've developed a method to deliver LATS1 mRNA directly into the eye using lipid nanoparticles. This approach involves using a virus-free technique to introduce LATS1 mRNA into uveal melanoma cells.

In their study, the researchers used two different types of lipid nanoparticles, SM102 and MC3, to deliver LATS1 mRNA to mice with uveal melanoma. They found that the SM102-based nanoparticles were more effective at getting the mRNA into the tumor cells and promoting the production of LATS1 protein. Specifically, they observed that the delivered mRNA was mainly detected in tumor cells, with some additional expression in Müller glial cells.

The results showed that delivering LATS1 mRNA using SM102-based lipid nanoparticles significantly suppressed tumor growth in these mice. In fact, the treatment was able to inhibit uveal melanoma cell proliferation in a dose-dependent manner. While these findings are promising, it's worth noting that delivering drugs directly into the eye can be challenging, and the authors don't discuss specific limitations or potential side effects in this abstract.

Overall, this study suggests that using lipid nanoparticles to deliver LATS1 mRNA directly into the eye could be a viable treatment strategy for uveal melanoma. By targeting the LATS1/YAP axis, this approach aims to restore normal cellular function and slow down or stop tumor growth. Further research would be needed to translate these findings into clinical practice.

**Abstract:** Uveal melanoma (UM) is a rare and highly aggressive intraocular malignancy, in which the key oncogenic driver Yes-associated protein (YAP) is persistently activated due to loss of upstream Hippo kinases, including Large Tumor Suppressor Kinase 1 (LATS1). The LATS1/YAP axis thus represents a critical therapeutic target, and restoring LATS1 activity offers a promising intervention strategy. In this study, we developed an mRNA-based therapeutic approach targeting the LATS1/YAP axis for UM treatment. Lentivirus-mediated overexpression confirmed that exogenous LATS1 suppresses YAP activity in UM cells. Delivery of in vitro transcribed LATS1 mRNA inhibited UM cell proliferation in a dose-dependent manner. To overcome ocular drug delivery challenges, we compared two lipid nanoparticle (LNP) formulations with different ionizable lipids SM102and MC3 for intraocular mRNA delivery. The results showed SM102-based LNPs exhibited superior mRNA translation efficiency compared to MC3-LNPs in an orthotopic UM mouse model. Intraocular distribution analysis further revealed that the delivered mRNA was predominantly detected in tumor cells, with additional expression observed in Müller glial cells. Finally, intravitreal administration of LATS1 mRNA-loaded SM102-LNPs demonstrated significant suppression to the orthotopic tumor growth. These findings establish mRNA-based restoration of LATS1 via LNPs as a promising therapeutic strategy for UM.

### Optoelectronic artificial synapse for lateral inhibition-enhanced retinal biomimicry.
_Yang M et al. — Nanotechnology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42127936/)

**Spoken:** This study from Qingdao University in China explores the development of an optoelectronic artificial synapse that mimics the human retina's processing of visual information. The motivation behind this work is to enhance edge detection and contrast resolution in complex visual scenes, which could have significant implications for machine vision systems and bioinspired robots.

The researchers designed an optoelectronic synaptic device that incorporates lateral inhibition, a neural mechanism that refines spatial selectivity and extends the dynamic range by suppressing redundant signals and amplifying subtle variations in input intensity. They created a novel heterostructure consisting of metal oxide, perovskite, and a thin film, which enables the emulation of neural behavior and advanced information processing.

The device simulates excitatory synaptic activity through light stimulation and mimics lateral inhibition through electrical stimulation, effectively replicating the neural mechanisms of synaptic plasticity. The researchers tested their device and achieved impressive results, including 97% accuracy in image recognition, surpassing conventional networks at 93%.

The clinical implications of this study are promising, with potential applications in developing highly efficient machine vision systems and intelligent autonomous devices. The authors suggest that their design could provide a cost-effective and energy-efficient route for directing robotic arms to perform responding motions. While limitations are not explicitly mentioned, the authors note that seamless integration with robotic arms enabled color chip recognition on a machine cart, highlighting the potential for bioinspired robots. Overall, this study presents an innovative approach to retinal biomimicry, with exciting possibilities for future applications.

**Abstract:** Optoelectronic synaptic devices enable in-sensor processing of enhanced edge detection and contrast resolution in complex visual scenes due to their excellent capability to emulate the functions of visual neurons, such as light perception and image processing , while lateral inhibition synaptic plasticity refines spatial selectivity and extends the dynamic range by suppressing redundant signals and amplifying subtle variations in input intensity. The incorporation of lateral inhibition into a single optoelectronic synaptic device will offer a cost-effective and energy-efficient route for directing a robotic arm to perform responding motions and developing highly efficient machine vision systems. Herein, we demonstrate an optoelectronic artificial synapse established on a novel heterostructure consisting of metal oxide In2O3,polycrystalline Cs2AgBiBr6 perovskite and indium-gallium-zinc oxide (IGZO) thin film, which enhances the optoelectronic response and corresponding synaptic plasticity of the devices, enabling the emulation of neural behaviour and advanced information processing. The structure simulates excitatory synaptic activity through light stimulation and mimics lateral inhibition through electrical stimulation, effectively replicating the neural mechanisms of synaptic plasticity in processes such as Mach bands, contrast enhancement, and Hermann's grid. Leveraging these properties, we develop a lateral inhibition network for image recognition, achieving 97% accuracy-surpassing conventional networks at 93%. Additionally, through seamless integration with robotic arms, it can execute colour chip recognition on a machine cart, providing a promising strategy for the design of intelligent autonomous devices and bioinspired robots.

However, most demonstrated benefits concentrate on ergonomics, workflow efficiency, and motion stability, with fewer studies showing consistent improvements in long-term clinical outcomes. Larger multicenter trials are needed to define true clinical value, cost-effectiveness, and optimal clinical integration.
