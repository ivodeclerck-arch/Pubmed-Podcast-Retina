**Spoken:** Here's a 300-word spoken audio segment about the research paper:

A 30-year-old woman presented with a new-onset headache, nausea, vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia, and right nasal hemianopsia. Her symptoms were nonspecific, making it challenging to pinpoint the cause.

The patient's ocular POCUS examination revealed a hyperechoic lesion adhered to the vascular layer that didn't move with eye movements. This finding was critical in suspecting a neoplastic lesion. The patient was subsequently referred to ophthalmology, where choroidal melanoma was confirmed. Choroidal melanoma is a malignant lesion of the uveal tract and is the most common ocular melanoma, particularly in younger women.

What's notable about this case is that ocular POCUS was able to complement the physical exam and fundoscopic evaluation, which are critical for diagnosis. The authors suggest that ocular POCUS is relatively easy to perform, given the eye's high liquid content, which provides an excellent medium for sound transmission. The timely diagnosis of intraocular neoplastic lesions is essential, as they are highly invasive and can metastasize to the liver and skin. In this case, enucleation was performed, and extension studies showed no metastases. The authors emphasize that ocular POCUS can be a valuable tool in the diagnostic approach to patients with nonspecific symptoms, allowing for the detection of non-mobile solid lesions that may suggest neoplasia. By facilitating early diagnosis, ocular POCUS can contribute to more effective management and treatment of patients with intraocular neoplasms.

**Abstract:** We report the case of a 30-year-old woman with no relevant medical history who presented with a new-onset headache associated with nausea, vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia, and right nasal hemianopsia. The patient was referred to ophthalmology, where choroidal melanoma was confirmed. Extension studies showed no metastases, and enucleation was performed. This case highlighted the importance of ocular POCUS in the diagnostic approach to this patient.

### One-Year Tumor Volume Regression Predicts Survival After I-125 Plaque Brachytherapy for Posterior Uveal Melanoma.
_Dave N et al. — American journal of ophthalmology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42128079/)

**Spoken:** This study from the Hamilton Eye Institute, Department of Ophthalmology, University of Tennessee, in Memphis, Tennessee, explores the prognostic significance of tumor volume regression after brachytherapy for posterior uveal melanoma. The goal here is to understand how the rate of tumor shrinkage after treatment can predict patient outcomes.

In uveal melanoma, or ocular cancer, finding reliable predictors of survival is crucial for guiding treatment decisions and patient counseling. Brachytherapy, specifically with iodine-125 episcleral plaque, is a common treatment approach. The question is, can the extent of tumor regression at one year post-treatment serve as a biomarker for overall survival?

The study retrospectively analyzed data from 1,180 patients with uveal melanoma who underwent low-dose-rate iodine-125 episcleral plaque brachytherapy at a large academic medical center between 1984 and 2022. Patients had ultrasound measurements of their tumors at diagnosis and one year after treatment. The researchers calculated tumor volume regression and categorized patients into groups based on the rate of regression: delayed, ≤33.3%; intermediate, 33.3-75%; and accelerated, ≥75%.

The key finding is that patients with intermediate tumor regression had better overall survival compared to those with delayed or accelerated regression. Notably, 94.4% of patients showed some degree of tumor volume reduction by one year, with a mean decrease from 336.3 mm³ to 129.5 mm³. The association between tumor regression rate and survival held even when tumors were stratified by size and clinical stage.

The authors suggest that assessing tumor volume regression at one year post-brachytherapy could be a practical tool for risk stratification and patient counseling. However, it's essential to consider the study's limitations, such as its retrospective design and reliance on ultrasound measurements. These findings support a more tailored approach to follow-up and treatment planning based on individual tumor response. For ophthalmologists, integrating this information into clinical practice could help refine patient care pathways.

**Abstract:** OBJECTIVE: To evaluate the prognostic significance of one-year tumor volume regression (TVR) following low-dose-rate iodine-125 episcleral plaque brachytherapy (EPBT) in patients with uveal melanoma. DESIGN: Retrospective single-center clinical cohort study. PARTICIPANTS: A total of 1,180 patients with uveal melanoma treated with low-dose-rate iodine-125 EPBT delivered at 52.8 cGy/h over 168 hours at a large academic medical center with an ocular oncology service between 1984 and 2022. Patients had tumor ultrasound measurements at diagnosis and one-year follow-up, with complete survival data available. Patients without complete survival data or ultrasound measurements at either timepoint were excluded. MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES: TVR was calculated using ultrasound-derived ellipsoid modeling at baseline and 1 year. Patients were stratified into delayed (≤33.3%), intermediate (33.3-75%), and accelerated (≥75%) regression groups. Thresholds were selected using a log-rank test sweep across -100% to 100% regression in conjunction with locally weighted scatterplot smoothing (LOWESS) analysis. Inflection points were identified at 33.3% (p = 0.029) and 75% (p = 0.001). Kaplan-Meier (KM) survival analysis was used to compare overall survival (OS) among regression groups, with significance assessed by log-rank tests. RESULTS: Mean tumor volume decreased from 336.3 mm³ to 129.5 mm³ by one year, with 94.4% (N=1,114) showing volume reduction. KM survival analysis demonstrated that patients with delayed and accelerated tumor regression had worse OS than those with intermediate-range regression across the full cohort (p < 0.0001, p < 0.0001). This association was conserved when tumors were stratified by Collaborative Ocular Melanoma Study (COMS) size and American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) clinical stage guidelines. No difference was seen between the intermediate and accelerated regression groups for COMS small and cT1 tumors. CONCLUSION: TVR at one year following EPBT is a significant prognostic biomarker for OS in UM, with intermediate regression (33.33% to 75%) conferring the greatest survival benefit. These findings support one year tumor regression metrics as a practical tool for risk stratification and patient counseling.

### Reassessing the incidence of uveal melanoma in Germany: Insights from current cancer registry data.
_Lever M et al. — Cancer epidemiology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42127869/)

**Spoken:** Here's a 300-word spoken audio segment based on the provided abstract:

Uveal melanoma, or UM, is a type of eye cancer that's most common in adults. It's a significant concern in Europe, and researchers from the Department of Ophthalmology at University Hospital Essen in Germany have been looking into its incidence in their country. The question is, how often does UM actually occur in Germany?

Calculating the incidence of UM can be tricky due to current diagnostic and therapeutic practices. To get a better handle on the numbers, the researchers analyzed data from the German Centre for Cancer Registry Data, or RKI, covering the years 2019 to 2021. They also compared this data to cancer registry information from North Rhine-Westphalia, or NRW, which is Germany's largest federal state.

The study included over 2,000 UM cases from Germany and around 830 cases from NRW. The average age at diagnosis was 65 years old, with men and women being equally affected. Interestingly, about 11-13% of cases involved melanomas of the ciliary body and iris.

The age-standardized incidence rate for UM in Germany was found to be 7.4 per million person-years. However, there were significant regional variations, with the highest rates seen in the North and North-Eastern federal states. For example, Schleswig-Holstein had an incidence rate of 16.6 per million person-years. In NRW, the rate was 6.2 per million person-years according to the RKI dataset, but 10.6 per million person-years according to the state's own cancer registry data.

So, what does this mean for clinicians? The study suggests that the incidence of UM in Germany might be higher than previously thought, and similar to rates seen in Northern Europe, which are around 10-12 per million person-years. However, the authors note that determining the incidence of UM in Germany remains challenging due to potential incompleteness of the RKI dataset. This highlights the need for more accurate and comprehensive data collection to inform our understanding of this condition.

**Abstract:** BACKGROUND: Uveal melanoma (UM), the most common adult primary intraocular malignancy, has its highest frequency in Europe. Because of the current diagnostic and therapeutic practices, calculating UM incidence can be error prone. Using cancer registry data, the aim here was to estimate UM incidence in Germany and North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW), its largest federal state. METHODS: All UM cases from the German Centre for Cancer Registry Data (RKI) in 2019-2021 were analyzed. Data were compared to the 2019-2022 data of NRW's cancer registry. We calculated crude incidence and age-standardized rates (ASR) using the 2013 European standard population. RESULTS: Overall, 2047 German and 830 NRW UM cases were included. In both datasets, the mean age at diagnosis was 65 years; men and women were equally affected. Ciliary body and iris melanomas represented 11-13% of total cases. In Germany (RKI dataset), ASR was 7.4 per million person-years (pyrs). Highest ASR was in the North (Schleswig-Holstein: 16.6 per million pyrs), followed by North-Eastern federal states (ASR: 11-13 per million pyrs in Brandenburg, Berlin, Saxony-Anhalt, Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania), and ASR was 4-8 per million pyrs in the remaining federal states. ASR in NRW was 6.2 (RKI dataset) but 10.6 per million pyrs using the state's cancer registry data. CONCLUSION: Determining UM incidence in Germany remains challenging. High incidence variations between both datasets and German federal states point to a probable incompleteness of the RKI UM-dataset. Incidence is likely higher than previously assumed, but similar to rates observed in Northern Europe (around 10-12 per million pyrs).

### Network-based analysis reveals potential microRNA regulation of oncogenic pathways in SOX10-depleted uveal melanoma.
_Luan C et al. — Cellular and molecular life sciences : CMLS_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42120529/)

**Spoken:** This study comes from the Department of Dermatology at Universitätsklinikum Erlangen and Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg in Erlangen, Germany. The clinical question here is about understanding the role of SOX10 in uveal melanoma, specifically how it regulates microRNAs, or miRNAs, which are small genes that help control other genes.

Uveal melanoma is a type of eye cancer that's quite aggressive, and SOX10 is a protein that's crucial for the development and maintenance of melanocytes, the cells from which this cancer originates. While we know SOX10 helps regulate protein-coding genes, its role in regulating miRNAs in uveal melanoma wasn't well understood.

To investigate this, the researchers used a network-based approach to analyze how SOX10 depletion affects the regulation of miRNAs in uveal melanoma cells. They did this by comparing SOX10 wild-type and knockdown cells, which means they looked at cells with normal levels of SOX10 and cells where SOX10 was artificially reduced.

The researchers found that SOX10 depletion leads to massive changes in the transcriptomic landscape of uveal melanoma cells, including changes in 2,222 up-regulated and 1,156 down-regulated protein-coding genes. They also identified 17 miRNAs that show significant changes in regulatory activities following SOX10 knockdown. Some of these miRNAs, like miR-34a, miR-25, miR-186, and miR-211, have tumor-suppressive potential by targeting genes involved in uveal melanoma progression and metastasis.

For instance, the researchers found that SOX10 knockdown downregulates several transcription factors, including MITF, which is involved in cell proliferation and survival. They also found that GATA2, TRIM33, and PMEPA1 exhibit significant increases in regulatory activity, while MYBL2, NEDD4L, and STUB1 exhibit substantial decreases.

The clinical implications of this study are that understanding how SOX10 regulates miRNAs in uveal melanoma could lead to new therapeutic targets. The authors note that their findings suggest that SOX10 depletion in uveal melanoma reshapes the miRNA regulatory landscape in a manner potentially consistent with tumor-suppressive activity. However, they also acknowledge that these findings require experimental validation.

It's worth noting that the authors mention some limitations of their study, including the fact that they used a cell line model, and that their findings may not directly translate to human disease. Additionally, they note that the regulatory activities of miRNAs can be complex and context-dependent, and that further studies are needed to fully understand the mechanisms underlying their findings.

**Abstract:** SOX10 is essential for melanocyte development and maintenance and plays a critical role in uveal melanoma (UM) initiation and progression. While SOX10's transcriptional regulation of protein-coding genes is well characterized, its role on microRNA (miRNA) regulatory landscape in UM remains unexplored. Here, we employed network-based modeling to systematically characterize miRNA regulatory functions following SOX10 depletion in UM. First, we profiled mRNA and miRNA expression levels in SOX10 wild-type and knockdown UM cells. Then, we integrated the transcriptomic data, a UM network, and a Bayesian model to quantify miRNAs' regulatory activities and identify key miRNAs. Subsequently, we employed pathway enrichment analysis combined with literature mining to elucidate the functional roles of identified miRNAs through their target genes and associated signaling pathways in UM. We identified 17 miRNAs that show significant changes in regulatory activities following SOX10 knockdown in UM cells. These miRNAs regulate the expression of genes involved in cancer hallmark pathways, including cell cycle progression, mTORC1 signaling, and fatty acid metabolism. Notably, miR-34a, miR-25, miR-186, and miR-211 have tumor-suppressive potential by targeting genes involved in UM progression and metastasis. Our computational analysis suggests that SOX10 depletion in UM reshapes the miRNA regulatory landscape in a manner potentially consistent with tumor-suppressive activity, requiring experimental validation.

### Long-Term Local Control and Mortality After Transpupillary Thermotherapy of Small Uveal Melanomas.
_Fili M et al. — Clinical & experimental ophthalmology_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42104514/)

**Spoken:** This study from the Department of Clinical Neuroscience, Division of Eye and Vision, Karolinska Institutet, Stockholm, Sweden, looks at the long-term effectiveness of transpupillary thermotherapy, or TTT, for treating small uveal melanomas. The goal here is to compare outcomes for patients who received TTT alone, TTT in combination with plaque brachytherapy, and those who had plaque brachytherapy alone.

The motivation behind this research is to assess whether TTT, often used for certain small choroidal melanocytic tumors, either on its own or alongside other treatments, provides sufficient long-term control over the tumor and impacts mortality rates. The study design involved a retrospective cohort analysis of 850 patients with choroidal melanocytic tumors who were treated at St. Erik Eye Hospital in Stockholm between 2008 and 2020.

Key findings show that at 15 years, the local recurrence-free survival rate was significantly different across the treatment groups: 8% for TTT alone, 34% for the combination of TTT and plaque brachytherapy, and 86% for those who received plaque brachytherapy alone. This trend was statistically significant. Moreover, disease-specific survival at 15 years varied, with 46% for TTT alone, 85% for the combination, and 90% for brachytherapy alone.

The study suggests that TTT alone was associated with a higher risk of local recurrence and melanoma-related death compared to plaque brachytherapy. Specifically, the subdistribution hazard ratio for local recurrence with TTT alone was 6.7, and for melanoma-related death, it was 3.9. 

The authors note that these findings imply TTT may not be the most effective standalone treatment for small uveal melanomas, suggesting a reconsideration of its role in treatment protocols. However, they also highlight the need for future studies to validate these results in external cohorts. A limitation mentioned is the observational nature of the study and the variability in treatment approaches over the study period. 

Overall, this research provides valuable insights into the long-term outcomes of TTT for small uveal melanomas, emphasizing the importance of careful treatment selection for optimal patient outcomes.

**Abstract:** BACKGROUND: Transpupillary thermotherapy (TTT) is used for selected small choroidal melanocytic tumours, either as primary treatment or as an adjunct to plaque brachytherapy. We compared long-term local recurrence and mortality outcomes after primary TTT alone, plaque brachytherapy combined with TTT (brachy+TTT), and plaque brachytherapy alone (brachy alone). METHODS: This retrospective cohort study included 850 patients with choroidal melanocytic tumours treated with eye-preserving modalities at St. Erik Eye Hospital, Stockholm, Sweden, 2008-2020. Primary treatment was TTT alone (n = 48), brachy+TTT (n = 105), or brachy alone (n = 697). Outcomes included local recurrence, local recurrence with extraocular extension (EXE), and mortality. RESULTS: At 15 years, Kaplan-Meier local recurrence-free survival was 8% after TTT alone, 34% after brachy+TTT, and 86% after brachy alone (log-rank test for trend, p < 0.001). Disease-specific survival at 15 years was 46%, 85%, and 90%, respectively (p = 0.03). In multivariable Fine-Gray regression adjusting for American Joint Committee on Cancer stage, TTT alone was associated with a higher subdistribution hazard of local recurrence (sHR, 6.7; p < 0.001) and melanoma-related death (sHR, 3.9; p < 0.001). Among patients treated for local recurrence, subsequent recurrences occurred more frequently after TTT than after treatments involving brachytherapy. The risk of EXE increased with the number of TTT sessions. CONCLUSIONS: In this single-centre retrospective cohort, TTT was associated with higher rates of local recurrence and melanoma-related mortality compared with plaque brachytherapy. Future studies should assess whether similar results are observed in external long-term cohorts; such findings would inform whether the role of TTT in the management of choroidal melanocytic tumours warrants reconsideration.

### Intravitreal delivery of LATS1 mRNA by lipid nanoparticles as an effective strategy for uveal melanoma therapy.
_Dong S et al. — Journal of controlled release : official journal of the Controlled Release Society_  
[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41786044/)

**Spoken:** Uveal melanoma, a rare but aggressive eye cancer, has a key driver of its growth that's been hard to target. The Yes-associated protein, or YAP, is overactive in this cancer because of a loss of certain brakes, like Large Tumor Suppressor Kinase 1, or LATS1. Restoring LATS1 could be a promising way to treat this cancer. A team from the Eye Research Center, Hangzhou Institute of Medicine, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and Wenzhou Medical University in China, has been exploring this idea.

They started by confirming that adding LATS1 to uveal melanoma cells in the lab suppresses YAP activity. Then, they used a special kind of RNA, called mRNA, that encodes for LATS1, and delivered it to the cells. They found that this approach inhibited the growth of the cancer cells in a dose-dependent way. To get the mRNA into the eye, they used lipid nanoparticles, which are tiny particles made of fat that can carry the mRNA into the eye.

The researchers compared two different types of lipid nanoparticles, and found that one, made with a lipid called SM102, worked better than the other. When they gave this treatment to mice with uveal melanoma, they saw significant suppression of the tumor growth. The delivered mRNA was mostly detected in the tumor cells themselves, with some additional expression in Müller glial cells.

The clinical implications of this study are promising. The authors suggest that delivering LATS1 mRNA via lipid nanoparticles could be a new way to treat uveal melanoma. However, it's worth noting that this is still an early-stage study, and more research is needed to translate these findings to the clinic. The authors don't mention any specific limitations, but it's likely that further studies will be needed to assess the safety and efficacy of this approach in humans.

**Abstract:** Uveal melanoma (UM) is a rare and highly aggressive intraocular malignancy, in which the key oncogenic driver Yes-associated protein (YAP) is persistently activated due to loss of upstream Hippo kinases, including Large Tumor Suppressor Kinase 1 (LATS1). The LATS1/YAP axis thus represents a critical therapeutic target, and restoring LATS1 activity offers a promising intervention strategy. In this study, we developed an mRNA-based therapeutic approach targeting the LATS1/YAP axis for UM treatment. Lentivirus-mediated overexpression confirmed that exogenous LATS1 suppresses YAP activity in UM cells. Delivery of in vitro transcribed LATS1 mRNA inhibited UM cell proliferation in a dose-dependent manner. To overcome ocular drug delivery challenges, we compared two lipid nanoparticle (LNP) formulations with different ionizable lipids SM102and MC3 for intraocular mRNA delivery. The results showed SM102-based LNPs exhibited superior mRNA translation efficiency compared to MC3-LNPs in an orthotopic UM mouse model. Intraocular distribution analysis further revealed that the delivered mRNA was predominantly detected in tumor cells, with additional expression observed in Müller glial cells. Finally, intravitreal administration of LATS1 mRNA-loaded SM102-LNPs demonstrated significant suppression to the orthotopic tumor growth. These findings establish mRNA-based restoration of LATS1 via LNPs as a promising therapeutic strategy for UM.

**Abstract:** Optoelectronic synaptic devices enable in-sensor processing of enhanced edge detection and contrast resolution in complex visual scenes due to their excellent capability to emulate the functions of visual neurons, such as light perception and image processing , while lateral inhibition synaptic plasticity refines spatial selectivity and extends the dynamic range by suppressing redundant signals and amplifying subtle variations in input intensity. **Abstract:** PURPOSE OF REVIEW: Rapid advances in surgical visualization, microsurgical instrumentation, artificial intelligence (AI), and robotic assistance are reshaping ophthalmic surgery.
